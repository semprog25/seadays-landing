AIDA Cruises Secrets: 10 Things Regular Guests Know (But Newcomers Don’t)

AIDA Is Not a “Typical” Cruise (And That’s the Advantage)

Photo: sharanestone

AIDA does things differently — and that’s why people love it.

Related: First Time Cruise Tips: What New Cruise Travelers Should Know · How to Keep Track of All Your Cruises (Without Forgetting the Details) · 7 Days of Pure Fun: Why a Mediterranean Cruise Is the Ultimate Feel-Good Trip · Cruise Packing Checklist: What Most Passengers Forget

What makes AIDA unique:

  • Casual atmosphere (no formal nights)
  • Open dining instead of fixed seating
  • Strong focus on wellness, sports, and freedom

Why this matters: If you expect a classic luxury cruise, you’ll miss what makes AIDA special. If you lean into the lifestyle, it’s one of the most relaxing cruise brands out there.

Choosing the Right AIDA Ship Changes Everything

Not all AIDA ships feel the same. Picking wrong can ruin your vibe.

Quick guide:

  • AIDAnova / AIDAcosma → modern, big, energy, families
  • AIDAprima / AIDAperla → balance of comfort & activity
  • AIDAluna / AIDAbella / AIDAvita → smaller, calmer, classic

Pro tip: If you hate crowds, avoid holiday sailings on Nova & Cosma. No exceptions.

Cabin Mistakes AIDA First-Timers Always Make

Cabin choice matters more than people admit.

Avoid these errors:

  • Booking directly under the Theatrium
  • Choosing aft cabins if you’re sensitive to vibration
  • Assuming “inside cabin = bad”

Truth: AIDA inside cabins are quiet, dark, and perfect if you spend your day outside anyway.

Dining on AIDA: How to Eat Better Without Paying More

Yes, AIDA food is included — but strategy matters.

Smart dining tips:

  • Buffet quality varies by time, not by restaurant
  • Early dinner = calmer, fresher food
  • Specialty restaurants are great, but not mandatory

Hidden win: Lunch on embarkation day is usually better than dinner. Most guests miss this.

Drink Packages & Costs: What’s Actually Worth It

AIDA drink packages confuse almost everyone.

Reality check:

  • Packages must be booked for all adults in the cabin
  • Sea days = higher value than port-heavy routes
  • Casual drinkers rarely break even

Rule: If you don’t drink 5–6 beverages per day, skip the package.

Shore Excursions: AIDA vs Independent Tours

AIDA excursions are reliable — but not always the best choice.

When to book with AIDA:

  • Short port stays
  • Tender ports
  • Remote destinations

When to go independent:

  • Big cities
  • Long port days
  • Repeat destinations

Never forget: If you’re late on your own tour, the ship leaves without you.

Sea Days on AIDA: How to Avoid Crowds

Sea days are where AIDA shines — if you’re smart.

Crowd-free tricks:

  • Gym & spa early morning
  • Pool decks during port days
  • Book shows right after embarkation

Best-kept secret: Sunrise on deck = silence, space, and unbeatable views.

Disembarkation on AIDA: End Strong

The last day decides how you remember the cruise.

Avoid these mistakes:

  • Late flights
  • Ignoring account checks
  • Packing too early

Golden rule: Never book a flight before 11:30 AM — especially in busy ports.
