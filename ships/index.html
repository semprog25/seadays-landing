Why a ships hub matters for cruise planning

Choosing a cruise is rarely only about price. The ship shapes daily life at sea: dining venues, cabin categories, entertainment, kids clubs, and how crowded common spaces feel. SeaDays brings structured ship and line information together so you can compare options without losing the story of what makes each vessel different.

Whether you are evaluating Royal Caribbean versus MSC for a family sailing, or researching Mediterranean embarkation ports before you lock flights, starting from the ship helps you align nights at sea with the regions you want to explore.

How to use this directory

Each card links to a static ship guide you can bookmark or share. For deck plans, live ship data, and community reviews, open the SeaDays app—download from the homepage and sign in to sync your preferences across devices.

For itinerary inspiration, pair this list with our cruise blog and the ports directory to sketch sea days, port days, and pre- or post-cruise stays.
